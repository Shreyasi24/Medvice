I live in the south and since summer started my baby has had a rash under their armpits and thighs. My baby has been very fussy and it looks uncomfortable. They have been very sweaty and irritable.

Babies are prone to what is called a ‘heat rash’ during warm, humid weather. Make sure they are wearing light natural fabrics and use fans and the A/C as needed. Remember you baby is not as good at thermo- regulating as you are. Please check with your doctor when you are able to get further information.

I have terrible migraines when I go outside and see bright lights. It’s hard to just walk around my neighborhood. What can I do?

I am sorry you are experiencing migraines. Fortunately, they are generally not dangerous. But they can be caused by many things such as bright lights (the sun), smells (trash, cheese, garlic), and noises (cars, construction). There are many kinds of migraines. Identifying the stimuli that cause your migraines and the type of migraine you have is important for treatment. Please see your doctor to discuss these symptoms and a course of treatment. I recommend seeing your doctor with a list of things that you know trigger you. Please also record your symptoms and how long they happen.

I am pre-diabetic and want to avoid getting worse. What is the best way fro me to do that?

It is great you’re taking control of your health. Diet is the first thing we typically recommend you modify. You can find information about the USDA recommendations called my plate at myplate.gov. Second, you should work with your doctor to consider how to incorporate physical activities and which are appropriate for you. It is important to discuss both with your doctor so you can protect yourself and prevent further issues.
